## 4. Style Brief

**Primary style:** Japandi / Wabi-sabi

Japandi blends Japanese minimalism (stillness, negative space, natural materials) with Scandinavian functionalism (warmth, light woods, practicality). Key principles:

- **Wood tones:** Ash, light walnut, or natural oak — avoid dark espresso or red mahogany
- **Fabrics:** Cotton, linen, or bouclé in off-white, clay, stone, or warm grey
- **Palette:** Neutral base (off-white walls → this matches Parc Esta's standard cream/white finish) + warm wood accents + one or two muted earthen tones
- **Forms:** Low-profile, clean lines, no ornate carvings or legs — platform beds, slatted bases, tapered legs on sofas and dining chairs
- **Feel:** Calm, uncluttered, livable — not sterile

**What to avoid:**

- Dark grey or navy heavy upholstery
- Shiny chrome or gold hardware
- Ornate/traditional European forms
- Overly industrial (raw concrete, black steel)

## 6. Coordination Principles

All three pieces must read as a cohesive set. Buying guide:

1. **Lock the wood tone first.** Ash and light walnut can coexist. Do not mix with red oak or dark wenge.
2. **One fabric colour family.** If sofa is oat linen, dining chair seats can be a slightly darker clay — same family.
3. **Vary texture, not colour.** Mix slatted wood (bed) + soft linen (sofa) + smooth wood (dining table) within the same neutral palette.
4. **Low profile throughout.** Platform bed + low sofa + round-legged dining table all reinforce the horizontal, grounded feel of Japandi.
